Deep Learning for Non-Invasive Intoxication Detection

Abstract of published paper: Current intoxication methods, though effective, often rely on intrusive procedures that require trained personnel or direct interaction with the individual, limiting their practicality for real-time applications [1], [2]. This study analyzes non-invasive approaches utilizing two Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) and a Quanvolutional Neural Network (QNN) trained on eye images to classify sobriety by analyzing visual cues such as pupil dilation, eye redness, and eyelid position. All models were trained and evaluated on a dataset of 1500 labeled images, achieving accuracies ranging from 68.65% to 99.74%.

Technologies Used: Python · TensorFlow/Keras · Quantum ML (QNN) · OpenCV · NumPy · Pandas

Key Features: Non-invasive intoxication detection using eye images,CNN and Quanvolutional Neural Network (QNN) models, Visual cue analysis (pupil dilation, redness, eyelid position)

Real-Time Weather Tracking System

Abstract of published paper: With over 320,000 new cases diagnosed annually worldwide, brain tumors present a significant global health challenge [1]. Despite the prevalence of these tumors, diagnosis often takes months to attain, leaving a significant lag in treatment initiation. Additionally, the high cost of neuroimaging and the rarity of many brain tumors contribute to a fragmented data landscape, where datasets are often too small or suffer from class imbalances, preventing the development of larger diagnostic models. This research explores how the addition of synthetic data can enhance classification accuracy in brain tumor diagnosis. This study investigates the impact of varying proportions of synthetic data, generated using SD-Turbo, on the classification performance of a ResNet50-based architecture. We tested ResNet50 across eight training conditions with increasing proportions of synthetic MRI tumor images. Our results show that moderate inclusion of synthetic data (up to 50%) led to performance degradation, whereas larger synthetic ratios (≥ 2×) consistently improved all classification metrics. Most notably, synthetic data augmentation at a 1:4 ratio boosted classification accuracy to 94.81%, a 1.3 percent gain over real-data training alone. These findings showcase the potential of integrating synthetic data in enhancing model performance. Our code implementation is publicly available on our GitHub.

Technologies Used: Python · PyTorch / TensorFlow · ResNet50 · SD-Turbo (Synthetic Data Generation) · NumPy · Pandas · Matplotlib

Key Features: Brain tumor classification from MRI scans, Synthetic data augmentation using SD-Turbo, ResNet50-based deep learning architecture, Evaluation across multiple real-to-synthetic data ratios

Comparative Analysis of CNNs and LLMs for Detection of Intoxication through Ocular and Facial Features Using Ensemble Learning

Drunk driving remains a significant cause of fatalities in the U.S., with one person dying every 39 minutes. Current intoxication detection methods are expensive, time-consuming, and often require specialized equipment and trained personnel [1]. We propose a non-invasive approach using facial image analysis to detect intoxication. Our results show that the VGG16 model, optimized with Adamax and trained on 10,000 facial images, achieved 83% accuracy in detecting inebriation. This method demonstrates strong potential as an AI-based facial intoxication detection system, offering a practical solution to combat drunk driving.

Technologies Used: Python · TensorFlow / Keras · VGG16 · Adamax Optimizer · OpenCV · NumPy · Pandas

Key Features: Non-invasive intoxication detection using facial imagery,CNN-based feature extraction from ocular and facial cues, VGG16 model optimized with Adamax
